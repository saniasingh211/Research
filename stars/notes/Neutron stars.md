## Nuclear Astrophysics — Neutron Stars, r-Process, and Heavy Elements

### The problem of heavy elements
Stars fuse hydrogen → helium → carbon → oxygen → ... → iron.
Iron is the most tightly bound nucleus — peak of the binding energy curve.
Fusing lighter elements releases energy — rolling downhill toward iron.
Fusing iron costs energy — pushing uphill. Stars die instead.

Elements 1-26 (up to iron): made inside stars.
Elements 27-118 (iron to uranium): need a different process.

### Neutron capture — the solution
Neutrons have no charge. No electrical repulsion. They slip into nuclei freely.
Nucleus absorbs neutron → gets heavier → sometimes unstable → beta decays:
$$ n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ $$
 

Neutron becomes proton. → Element number increases by one.
→ Absorb another neutron. →  Decay again. **Climb the periodic table.**

### Two speeds — s and r process
**s-process (slow):** one neutron at a time, time to decay between captures.
Happens inside stars. Makes elements up to bismuth (83). Well understood.

**r-process (rapid):** neutrons captured faster than decay can happen.
Pile on neutrons → extremely neutron-rich unstable nuclei → 
all decay at once → cascade to stable heavy elements.
Makes gold, platinum, uranium — all heavy elements above bismuth.

Needs neutron density ~10²⁴ per cm³.
Best human nuclear reactors: ~10¹⁵ per cm³.
Nine orders of magnitude away from r-process conditions.

### Where the r-process happens
Only two candidates:
- Core collapse supernovae — maybe, still debated
- Neutron star mergers — confirmed 2017

### GW170817 — the confirmation
August 17, 2017. LIGO + Virgo detect gravitational waves.
Signal lasts ~100 seconds. Masses: 1.1 and 1.6 solar masses → neutron stars.

How we knew it was neutron stars and not black holes:
1. Masses in neutron star range from chirp frequency
2. Tidal deformability — neutron stars deform each other tidally,
   black holes don't. Deformation signature visible in late inspiral.

1.7 seconds after merger → Fermi detects gamma ray burst.
That 1.7 second gap confirms gravitational waves and light travel 
at the same speed to 1 part in 10¹⁵. General relativity confirmed.

70 telescopes on 7 continents pointed at NGC 4993, 130 million light years away.
Kilonova detected within 11 hours. Spectrum showed strontium.
Light curve matched r-process radioactive decay perfectly.

**The debate ended. Neutron star mergers make the heavy elements.**

### The numbers
One merger produces several Earth masses of gold and platinum.
Every heavy atom on Earth was made in a neutron star merger
before the solar system formed — scattered into space,
incorporated into the gas cloud that became the sun and Earth,
concentrated by geology, mined by humans.

Every gram of gold is a fossil of a cosmic collision billions of years ago.

### What's still open
- Supernovae may contribute too — especially early universe
- Exact nuclear physics of r-process nuclei — many never made in lab
- FRIB (Michigan) building unstable nuclei one atom at a time
- Equation of state of neutron stars affects how much gold gets made
- Everything connects: interior physics → ejected mass → r-process yield → gold abundance

### The multimessenger timeline — GW170817
1. Billions of years — two neutron stars spiral together
2. T+0s — 100 seconds of gravitational waves, LIGO detects
3. T+1.7s — gamma ray burst, Fermi detects
4. T+11hrs — kilonova detected optically
5. T+days — spectrum shows strontium, r-process confirmed
6. T+weeks — kilonova fades as heavy elements decay
7. T+months — X-ray and radio afterglow

One event. Every messenger. Most information ever from one observation.
