## What we did
Queried GALAH DR3 catalogue — 1000 stars with clean 
spectroscopic flags. Hunted for chemical outliers by 
barium and europium abundance.

## The star — GALAH 131120002001243
- Distance: 35,000 pc — halo star
- Radial velocity: 122 km/s — unusual, not a disk star
- [Fe/H] = -0.54 — moderately old
- Ba: 84x solar
- Eu: 12x solar
- La: 10x solar
- Ce: 24x solar
- Sm: 21x solar
- Every r-process element at least 10x solar simultaneously

## Key concepts learned
- [X/Fe] is logarithmic: 1.0 dex = 10x solar
- Iron tracks stellar generation — low iron = old star
- Ba has two production channels: r-process and s-process
- Eu is purely r-process — cleaner tracer
- Stellar atmosphere locks in birth chemistry permanently
- Stars are chemical fossils of the gas cloud they formed from

## What the plots showed
1. **R-process profile** — every heavy element elevated, 
   Ba highest at 1.92 dex
2. **Ba distribution** — our star sits completely alone 
   at the extreme tail of 1000 stars
3. **Ba vs Eu scatter** — no correlation across population, 
   confirms dual production channels for Ba
4. **Metallicity vs Eu** — clear downward trend: older 
   stars more Eu enriched. Our star sits above the trend 
   at its iron level — anomalously enriched for its age
5. **Sky position** — found one potential birth sibling 
   0.22 degrees away, same age, similar Eu, very different Ba

## Interpretation
Our star formed from a gas cloud polluted by a neutron 
star merger. The merger predates the star — the europium 
is second generation r-process material at minimum.

The neighbour star shares the Eu enrichment but not the 
Ba — suggesting both formed from the same merger-enriched 
cloud, but our star had additional exposure to s-process 
enrichment from a nearby AGB star.

**Our star (white):**

- Highest barium of all — starts at 1.92, drops sharply to La, recovers at Ce, holds steady through Sm and Eu
- Uniquely high Ba relative to everything else

**The pink stars split into two groups:**

**Group 1 — top cluster:** Several stars tracking high across all elements, staying above 1.2-1.5 throughout. These are consistently r-process enriched across the whole chain.

**Group 2 — bottom cluster:** Stars that have high Ba but then drop sharply — Ce near zero or below, Eu low. These are probably s-process dominated — barium from AGB stars, not neutron star mergers. The europium didn't come with it.

**The one that dips below zero at Ce** — that's interesting. High barium, sub-solar cerium. Unusual pattern, worth investigating separately.

Our star sits between the two groups in shape but is the extreme outlier in Ba amplitude.

**The scientific conclusion:** Not all high-Ba stars are the same. Some are r-process enhanced. Some are s-process dominated. The profile shape is the discriminator — flat and high across all elements means r-process, peaked at Ba and dropping means s-process.
