
Iron translates to a star's age. Less Iron : early formation. More Iron: later formation. 
It's almost like water pollution levels. the level of iron in a star tells us how much long its been polluting. (living)
# The Chain: 

- Two neutron stars orbit each other, slowly spiralling inward over billions of years
- They merge — enormous r-process explosion, europium and barium and lanthanum scattered into surrounding gas
- That gas cloud sits there, heavily enriched
- Later, our star forms from that cloud — inheriting the full chemical blast
- Our star is younger than the merger event that enriched it
---

## **What we did** 
Queried GALAH DR3 — 1000 stars with clean spectroscopic flags. Searched for chemical outliers by barium abundance. Found 11 stars with [Ba/Fe] > 1.0. Most extreme: GALAH 131120002001243.

## **The star**

- Distance: 35,000 pc — halo star, edge of Milky Way
- Radial velocity: 122 km/s — unusual, not a disk star
- [Fe/H] = -0.54 — metal poor, old
- Ba: 84x solar, Eu: 12x solar, La: 10x solar, Ce: 24x solar, Sm: 21x solar
- Every r-process element elevated by at least 10x simultaneously

## **What this means** 

Systematic enhancement across entire r-process chain = star formed from gas polluted by a neutron star merger before it was born. Single enrichment event, locally dominant.

**What we learned**

- [X/Fe] is logarithmic. 1.0 dex = 10x solar, 2.0 dex = 100x solar
- Iron is the reference because it has hundreds of measurable lines and tracks stellar generation
- Low iron = old star, formed before many supernovae had enriched the galaxy
- Ba has two production channels (r and s process) — Eu is the cleaner r-process tracer
- Ba/Eu not correlated across population — confirms dual production channels for Ba

**Open questions**

# Analogy


- Fresh water = early universe gas, almost no iron, no heavy elements
- Over time supernovae pollute it with iron — normal, expected, gradual
- **Our star formed from water that had normal iron pollution for its time**
- But it also had a **purple dye — europium — that shouldn't be there in that concentration at that stage**
- Purple dye doesn't come from the gradual iron pollution process
- It comes from a **single violent event** — a neutron star merger nearby
- One merger, one explosion, one local gas cloud completely drenched in r-process elements
- Our star formed from that drenched cloud
- It carries the chemical fingerprint of that explosion in its atmosphere forever

That neutron star merger happened billions of years ago, probably before our solar system existed. The star has been travelling through the halo of the Milky Way ever since, carrying the evidence inside it. We just read it today from a spectrum.

That's what spectroscopy is. Reading the universe's history from light.
So the source — the neutron star merger — is older than our star. And neutron stars themselves are remnants of massive stars that lived and died even before that. So the full chain goes back very deep in cosmic time.

The europium in our star is **second generation** r-process material at minimum. Possibly third. Each step older than the last.
